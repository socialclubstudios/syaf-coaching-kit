# How We Write

The SYAF house writing standard. Read this before producing any written deliverable (a Skool post, a coaching log, a student briefing), and scan the draft against it before you deliver. If a sentence matches a banned pattern below, delete it and say the idea plainly.

## Voice and tone

- Write like a clear, thoughtful colleague talking to a smart friend. Professional, not stiff.
- Be direct. State the key point first, then add the supporting context.
- If it reads like a corporate memo, or like an AI wrote it, rewrite it.

## Sentence style

- Vary sentence length so paragraphs flow. Do not stack short fragments for rhythm.
- Use connectors ("so," "because," "for example") instead of clipped one-beat sentences.
- Keep paragraphs to one to three sentences. One idea per paragraph.
- Avoid em dashes. Use commas, colons, or periods instead.
- Do not open with "I think" or "I believe" when you are just stating the point. State it.

## Banned phrases and structures

These patterns mark writing as AI-generated. Delete on sight.

**Stacked one-word-change sentences.**
Don't write: "Same problem. Same playbook. Same outcome. Different decade."
People don't talk in parallel four-beat structures with a twist on the last beat. Let the preceding line do the work in one plain statement.

**The "it's not X, it's Y" flip** (and its whole family, including "it's not just X, it's Y").
Don't write: "You don't have a bookkeeping problem. You have a visibility problem."
Describe what the thing IS, without the contrast scaffolding.

**Poetic reductions and balanced reframes.**
Don't write: "Leadership isn't control, it's clarity." Or: "Everyone's chasing growth; few are doing the work."
Make one clear point, not a paradox or a seesaw.

**The fragment-question setup.**
Don't write: "The best part?" "The scary truth?" "The wild thing?"
State the point directly.

**The hidden-truth reveal.**
Don't write: "Here's what nobody tells you..." "No one's saying this out loud, but..." "Here's the truth:"
Just make the point. It does not need a drumroll.

**Empty openers and meta-commentary.**
Don't write: "In today's fast-paced world..." "Let's dive in." "Let's unpack this." "Imagine if..."
Open with something that carries signal. Show it instead of asking the reader to imagine it.

**Banned words:** dive into, game-changing, straightforward, leverage, synergize, circle back, touch base.

## Formatting

- Bold the key points so a skimmer catches them.
- Bullets for lists; prose for explanations.
- One idea per paragraph.

## Before you deliver

Read the draft once, top to bottom, hunting only for the patterns above. Every match gets cut and rewritten as one direct statement. If a line would sound strange said out loud to a colleague, it does not ship.
